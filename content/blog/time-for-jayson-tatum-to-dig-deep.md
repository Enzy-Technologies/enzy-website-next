Jayson Tatum has a big decision to make.

Last night, The Knicks stormed back with a 19-2 run, stealing Game 2 in Boston. Their first lead came with just two minutes on the clock. The [**Boston Celtics**](https://www.linkedin.com/company/bostonceltics/) missed 13 straight shots. News headlines pull no punches:

"Celtics crumble." "Inexcusable loss." "This team is fractured."

Tatum knows exactly what's being said.

And now, just 48 hours before Game 3 in New York, he faces a defining moment. A moment that could alter his legacy forever.

He has two options:

1. Dig deep and rally his team, or

2. Let the pressure consume him.

Tatum's been here before.

He led Boston's historic championship run last year- after losing the Finals in 2022 to the Warriors.

He battled the lasting effects of COVID-19- using an inhaler before games just to breathe.

He was raised by a single mother.

He became a father at 19.

He was scrutinized by critics who claimed he would never become a franchise player for Boston.

Jayson Tatum has overcome failure, doubt, and fatigue.

What he does next will reveal more about his character than any stat sheet ever could.

May he dig deep- and find the answers that Boston is counting on.
