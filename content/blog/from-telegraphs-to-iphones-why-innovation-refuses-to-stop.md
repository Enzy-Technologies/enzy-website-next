Innovation doesn't happen overnight- it compounds over decades, even centuries.

Take the iPhone. Since its launch in 2007, [**Apple**](https://www.linkedin.com/company/apple/) has released new versions nearly every year- each one improving upon the last. Faster processors. Sharper cameras. Smarter messaging.

Today's iPhone is lightyears ahead of where it started. But it's only one chapter in the larger story of communication innovation.

150 years ago, rooms full of operators translated Morse code telegrams by hand- maybe a few hundred messages a day. Today, over 100 billion messages are sent daily.

That's the compounding effect of iteration. Small upgrades. Huge results.

Sales is no different.

Just twenty years ago, door to door reps scribbled addresses and notes in a paper route log. Called techs manually to share service details. It worked- but it was slow.

The first game-changer was automating scheduling and canvassing, changing the industry forever. But for too long, we stopped there.
